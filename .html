RULES

You can only move one checker one diagonal space forward during a regular turn.

Once the player with white checkers makes their first move, the player with black checkers moves, and then you will take turns.

If your checker is in the diagonal space nearest to one of your opponent's checkers, then you can jump and capture that checker.

To capture a checker, move two diagonal spaces in the direction of the checker you are attacking.

If you have the opportunity to jump your checker of opponent, you do not have to jump it.

To crown a checker and make it a king checker, simply place one of your own captured pieces on top of it.

The king can move forward and backward diagonally on the dark squares, so it is easier for king checkers to capture your checkers of opponent.

Continue jumping and capturing your checkers of opponent until they are all removed from the board.

Once you have captured all of your checkers of opponent, you have won the game.
